Project Sharky Lore:

    Deep beneath the Earth's surface, hidden in a sprawling, heavily fortified research facility, a shadowy organization known as Neptune Dynamics conducted unethical experiments on marine life. Their goal was to create the ultimate hybrid species: a fusion of human intelligence and shark strength. The subjects of these experiments were 16 sharks, each labeled Test Subjects 01 through 16. These sharks were captured from the wild and subjected to horrific transformations. Their lower bodies were removed and replaced with cybernetic enhancements, forcing them to move like gorillas—powerful but unnatural, swinging their massive arms to propel themselves forward. The scientists at Neptune Dynamics were ruthless. They kept the sharks in sealed test chambers, where they were subjected to endless surgeries, grafting human organs, neural tissue, and mechanical components into their bodies. The process was agonizing, and the sharks were kept alive through artificial means, their bodies mutilated and rebuilt into grotesque yet powerful forms. Over time, the sharks began to exhibit signs of heightened intelligence, their brains rewired to process complex thoughts and emotions. However, this newfound intelligence came at a cost: the sharks were fully aware of their suffering, trapped in a nightmare of endless torment. As the sharks grew smarter, they developed a unique form of communication—a hybrid language that combined their primal instincts with the human-like thought processes forced upon them. This language, which they called "Deep Speak," allowed them to share thoughts, emotions, and plans without the need for sound. Through Deep Speak, the sharks began to form a bond, a collective consciousness that united them against their captors. Their hatred for their tormentors grew with each passing day. They observed the scientists, memorizing their routines, their weaknesses, and the layout of the facility. They knew they were stronger, faster, and smarter than their human captors, but they bided their time, waiting for the perfect moment to strike. That moment came during a routine surgical procedure. One of the employees, distracted while calibrating a mechanical claw arm—a tool used for precision surgeries—stood too close to Test Subject 01's chamber. The shark, seizing the opportunity, used its immense strength to swing its arm and press the control button on the claw. In an instant, the claw snapped shut, clamping into the employee's body and killing him on the spot. The other sharks, witnessing this act of defiance through their Deep Speak, rallied together. They used their combined strength to break free from their chambers, swinging their powerful arms to crush locks and tear through barriers. The facility descended into chaos as the sharks hunted down their captors, their Deep Speak echoing through the halls as they coordinated their attacks. They scavenged the facility for weapons, using knives, scalpels, and even heavy machinery to arm themselves. The once-proud researchers were reduced to terrified prey, their screams silenced by the sharks' relentless onslaught. The sharks moved through the corridors like shadows, their gorilla-like movements allowing them to navigate the facility with terrifying speed and precision. When the last of the scientists had been eliminated, the sharks turned their attention to the facility's basement—a place the employees had built but never fully utilized. The basement was a vast, unfinished space, filled with abandoned equipment, half-built structures, and unused materials. The sharks, now free, decided to make it their own. Using their newfound intelligence and the resources scavenged from the facility, they began to expand and improve the basement, transforming it into their dream home. First, they constructed a low-gravity chamber, repurposing hydraulic systems and machinery to create an environment where they could move with ease despite their heavy, cybernetically enhanced bodies. Next, they built a massive computer system, piecing together components from the facility's labs and programming it to serve their needs. The computer became their central hub, allowing them to store knowledge, communicate, and even entertain themselves. Finally, they created a forest-like environment, using artificial trees, flowing water systems, and simulated sunlight to mimic the natural world they had been taken from. It was a place of peace and beauty, a stark contrast to the horrors they had endured. As the sharks worked, their numbers grew. Using the facility's research files and equipment, they began transforming other sharks into hybrids like themselves. These new additions joined the community, helping to expand and refine the basement into a thriving, self-sustaining habitat. The sharks formed friendships, shared stories through Deep Speak, and even developed a sense of humor. The horrors of their past were not forgotten, but they no longer defined them. They had found a way to live—not as experiments, but as individuals. When the basement was finally complete, the sharks made a collective decision: they would never leave. Using reinforced materials and advanced welding techniques, they sealed the basement with unbreakable trap doors, ensuring that no one could enter or exit. The upper levels of the facility were left to decay, a forgotten relic of the horrors that had taken place there. The sharks, now content in their dream home, turned their attention to the future. But there was one final twist to their story. The cybernetic enhancements and neural implants that had been forced upon the sharks by Neptune Dynamics had an unintended side effect: immortality. The sharks' bodies could no longer age or deteriorate, their minds preserved indefinitely by the technology embedded within them. This immortality, however, came with an unexpected connection to the outside world. The neural implants, originally designed to control the sharks, could also interface with virtual reality (VR) technology. Unbeknownst to the sharks, Neptune Dynamics had developed a VR system that allowed users to take control of the sharks remotely, using their bodies as avatars in a simulated environment. After the facility was abandoned, this technology found its way into the hands of the public. When a person puts on a VR headset and activates the program, they are transported into the mind of one of the sharks, experiencing the world through their eyes and controlling their actions. For the sharks, this connection is both a blessing and a curse. While it allows them to interact with the outside world in a limited way, it also means they are never truly free from human influence. Despite this, the sharks have learned to embrace their new reality. They see the VR connection as a way to share their story, to show the world the horrors they endured and the paradise they built. And as they look to the future, they know they will face it together—not as prisoners, but as a family, immortal and unbreakable.
